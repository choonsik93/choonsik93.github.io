JaeGoo Choy

I am an EIR at Carbon Black Group, where I explore how computer vision can create real-world impact. My research interests center on 3D scene understanding, articulated object modeling, and robot learning — building systems that enable machines to perceive and interact with the physical world.

Previously, I was a Research Scientist at Sequor Robotics, where I developed vision-based 3D mapping and robotic perception pipelines. I received my Ph.D. from Seoul National University, advised by Prof. Songhwai Oh, focusing on unsupervised articulated object understanding from 3D point clouds and neural radiance fields.

Multiscale Octree Voxel Mapping from Monocular Video [webpage]
JaeGoo Choy | Sequor Robotics

We build multiscale octree voxel maps from a single monocular video. From the captured scene, we first construct an octree image feature voxel map, then decode it to produce a color octree voxel map and a semantic octree voxel map.
